About me

I am a full stacked software developer from Hong Kong and based in London, UK. I enjoy writing codes and building side projets in my own time among other hobbies. It means a lot to me that I get to enjoy what we are doing at work, be passionate about learning new skills and improving ourselves, but most importantly be excited and proud of our products. This is how we can get off our bed every morning and start our day with energy and dedication. That is probably why I decided to work in the broadcast media industry and later the entertainment industry. Yes there are challenges and difficult times just like many other things in life, but on the weekends lying on my favourite yoga mat, I do feel satisfied and grateful as I know I have learnt a few things and have spent my time to work on something meaningful. Later at night there is no better sleep than that.

On a few occasions I was forunate enough to have the opportunities to travel to Singapore and Spain for work. Not only have I picked up Angular 2 and learnt a lot more about the business operation on the other side of the company, I have also made a few great friends in the trip and have some amazing experiences with different cultures, which I guess I really can't ask for more and makes my role as a software developer a lot more meaningful than just another day job.

In my spare time apart from coding, I like to play music, mostly on piano and drums. Sometimes after work I record a few cover songs with friends and share on our Youtube channel, usually are songs that we love and we always try to have our own interpretation on the original music, to play it in our own way that we think it should be. This is probably one of my most precious hobbies that not only I enjoy a lot, but also a space that I get to express myself in a way that normally cannot. It enhances our friendships and improves ourselves. Music is easily the best thing happens in my life, it gives so much and I always feel grateful and blessed.

After a long day of hard work, I sometimes sit down in front of my electric piano keyboard that lies next to a white and empty wall, and start playing anything that comes to mind. You can feel that one side of your mind is starting to get relaxed and the other side is activating. It is a wonderful and magical experience that fascinates me, almost like a yoga for your mind which brings a bit of peacefulness. I also believe that it brings a good balance to my life, the realisation of playing music and writing codes can actually complement each other quite well.
